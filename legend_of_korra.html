The Legend of Korra (2012)

TLOK is the successor of the critically-acclaimed series Avatar: The Last Airbender. It centers around the main protagonist, Korra, who is struggling to live up to the legacy of the previous avatar, Aang. She wrestles with her desperation for a purpose, especially as different antagonists convince her she doesn't have one. The series has four seasons, each dedicated to their separate villains, all with their own special parallels to Korra. Season One features Amon, the leader of the radical anti-bender group of 'Equalists'. While non-benders have made several attempts in the past to revolt against the harsh and unfair system promoting bender-superiority, they have all failed. The quality that sets Amon apart is his excellence at persuading/manipulating people. He is mystifying, charismatic, and most importantly, qualified. He has somehow managed to find a way to expel benders of their abilities, and this earns him the city's favor and trust. However, while this miracle may seem like a good thing for the Equalist movement, it eventually reveals itself to be something much more sinister, adding complexity to his character. Season Two introduces Unalaq, the chief of the Northern and Southern Water tribes, also known as Korra's uncle.
